About Me

I'm a Physics BSc student at LMU Munich with a research focus on high-energy cosmology and multi-messenger astronomy. My work explores the fundamental nature of our Universe, from dark energy and the Hubble tension to gamma-ray bursts and gravitational waves.

In 2024, I founded the CAPIBARA Collaboration, a student-led initiative to develop CubeSat missions for studying high-energy cosmic phenomena. I'm also passionate about science communication and education, regularly volunteering at science museums and mentoring young researchers.

Interests: Cosmology, Gamma-Ray Bursts, Multi-Messenger Astronomy, Data Science
Education: B.Sc. Physics, LMU Munich (Current)
Location: Munich, Germany

Research Interests

Multi-Messenger Cosmology

Combining electromagnetic, gravitational wave, and particle data to study cosmological parameters and the expansion history of the Universe.

Gamma-Ray Bursts

Using GRBs as cosmological probes and studying their physics through multi-wavelength observations and theoretical modeling.

Hubble Tension

Investigating the discrepancy in Hubble constant measurements through alternative distance ladder approaches.

Space Instrumentation

Developing compact, student-led satellite missions for high-energy astrophysics observations.
