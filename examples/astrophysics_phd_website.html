About Me

Welcome! I'm a PhD student in Astrophysics at [University Name], working under the supervision of [Advisor Name]. My research focuses on the computational modeling of black hole mergers and their gravitational wave signatures.


I'm passionate about understanding some of the most extreme phenomena in our universe, from the event horizons of supermassive black holes to the ripples in spacetime they create when they collide. My work combines theoretical physics with cutting-edge computational methods to simulate these cosmic events.


When I'm not analyzing data from LIGO or running simulations on supercomputers, you can find me stargazing, teaching undergraduate physics, or working on science outreach programs to share the wonder of astrophysics with the broader community.
