Cepheid Cosmology Research

Winter 2023
Advisors: Dr. Ignasi Pérez-Ràfols, Dra. Laia Casamiquela

As part of the first year of the Youth and Science Fellowship, I wrote a simple paper on computing the Hubble-Lemaître constant with Cepheid data from the archive. I recalculated H0 using the same methods as Edwin Hubble with modern data, yielding a value of 70.26 km s^-1 Mpc^-1. This value is consistent with previous estimates and aligns with the Big Bang theory of an expanding Universe.

    Recomputations of the Hubble-Lemaître constant using distance ladder methods, reflecting on the current Crisis in Cosmology
    J. Alcaide-Núñez (2025)
    Presented to as final examination in addition to oral discussion at Deutsche Schule Barcelona.

    Abstract: This study measures the Hubble-Lemaître constant (H₀) using Cepheid variables and Type Ia supernovae, finding values consistent with each other but noting a low statistical significance in the supernova data. The results contribute to the ongoing investigation into the Hubble Tension, the discrepancy between local and early-universe measurements of the expansion rate.

    Recomputing the Hubble Constant
    J. Alcaide-Núñez (2023)
    Presented to the Youth and Science Program

    Abstract: The Hubble constant has been derived multiple times, however, it cannot be agreed on a specific value. We recompute this value applying the same methods as Hubble did with more modern data. Making use of Cepheid variable stars as distance indicators and redshift for calculating radial velocities. We recover a value of 70.26 km s^-1 Mpc^-1 as the Hubble constant. We discuss the reuslts and relate them to the Big Bang theory of an expanding Universe, its implications in mathematical proofs, and further cosmological calculations of the Hubble parameter and the deceleration constant. Finally, we compare our results with previous calculations and consider our value consistent with previous estimates, as for the gravitational wave measurement.
