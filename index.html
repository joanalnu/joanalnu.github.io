About Me

I'm a Physics BSc student at LMU Munich and a passionate student researcher. I'm particularly interested in cosmology and the challenge of dark energy, the mysterious force accelerating the expansion of the Universe. In 2024, I created the CAPIBARA Collaboration, a group of high school students aiming to launch CubeSats to study the high-energy cosmos in its particle and light states. Additionally, I'm also a skilled programmer, demonstrated through work such as the Gen10 project and this website, which I coded from scratch.

A series of asynchronous materials and two online discussions on gravity (general relativity) and black holes. We also got to chat with PI researchers and ask them questions about their job! Image by Perimeter Institute.

International Research Stay on Multi-Messenger Cosmology (Italy)

Completed a 5-week research stay at the Osservatorio Astronomico di Brera, Sede di Merate (OAB-INAF) within the High Energy Astrophysics Group (HEA) on multi-messenger cosmology with the Epeak-Eiso relation and distance moduli from a simulated populations BNS mergers detectable by Einstein Telescope.
