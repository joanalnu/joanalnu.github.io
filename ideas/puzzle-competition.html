Problema 02

The Puzzle Competition Entrance

There is a puzzle competition with the following entry rules:

  • only groups of 3 people can pass
  • the sum of the ages of the group members, times 7 must be stricly smaller than 1000

    • There are 3 friends willing to enter the competition. Unfortunately they don't know their ages or their birthdays. But they know the following information:

  • Alice is twice as old as Bob, plus 6 years
  • Bob is one third as old as Gabriel, plus 2 years
  • Gabriel's age is given by the first two prime numbers multiplied, squared, plus 3 years

    • Are they able to enter the puzzle competition?

    With very elementary mental calculation skills (sum, multiplication, basic division) there is no need for the use of a computing machine (i.e., pocket calculator, computer).

    Are they young enough?

    Solution

    The first prime number is 2 and the second is 3. (2*3)^6 +3 = 6^2 + 3 = 36 + 3 = 39, this is the age of Gabriel.

    Bob is then 39/3 + 2 = 13 + 2 = 15

    Alice is then 15 * 2 + 6 = 36

    The sum of their ages times 7 is (36 + 15 + 39 ) * 7 = 90 * 7 = 630 < 1000

    That means they are allowed to enter the puzzle competition.
